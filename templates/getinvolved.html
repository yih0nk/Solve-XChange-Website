You can contribute to SolveXchange in numerous ways, helping to inspire transformative change in Halton and enhance the lives of students throughout our community.

Join in an ongoing discussion to share your ideas for improving various aspects of Halton. Learn about your peers' experiences and contribute your thoughts to a live forum.

Have an idea to fix an issue you experience in Halton? Sign up to enter the community-wide competition and have the chance to bring your idea to life with actual implementation.

Apply to become a part of our executive team and play a pivotal role in the operational framework of SolveXchange.

Feel Free to reply to the comments of others. Please be respectful.

Are you passionate about leadership? Take the initiative to establish a SolveXchange chapter at your high school! Join our expanding executive team and make a difference in your community by introducing SolveXchange to your peers.

SolveXchange presents a unique opportunity to align your brand with a socially impactful cause, gaining exposure while contributing to positive change in the Halton community. Play an integral role in bringing students' ideas to life, by supporting and empowering future leaders to inspire change throughout Halton.

What issues do you find present within your community? Have you thought of a solution to a problem you experience as a member of Halton? Share your ideas through a community-wide forum to hear the opinions of others, and further expand your plan. See what others have to share and collaborate with your peers!

Learn how to craft a professional business proposal and how to effectively market and promote your idea through detailed lessons. Join weekly google meetings to share your ideas, gain feedback and ask questions to ensure your success throughout the competition.

Eligibility

  • Must be a student attending a high school in Halton
  • Within the grades of 9-12
  • An HDSB email address and account
  • Can be completed individually or in small groups (1-4 people)

    • Rubric Criteria

    You will be graded based off:
  • Feasibility/Viability
  • Creativity/Innovative
  • Professionalism
  • Value of Proposition (benefits and impact)
  • Sustainability
  • Clarity
  • Visual Appeal
  • Implementation Plan

    Below are available positions if you are interested in becoming a part of the executive team:
  • Secretary
  • Grade 9 Rep
  • Grade 10 Rep
  • Promotions Team
  • Event/Competition Coordinator
  • Head of Communications
  • Business/Marketing Trainer

    • How to start your own chapter

    1.  Reach out and let us know that you want to start a chapter at your high school.

    2.  Depending on your school's club creation process, begin by submitting a request to start a club.

    3  Find a teacher supervisor to support the competition.

    4.  Meet with your principal and your school's administration team to determine if it would be possible for a
         winning solution to be actually implemented within the school.

    5.  Establish a team of passionate individuals to make up your own executive team.

    6.  Promote your chapter of SolveXchange throughout your school (ex. create an Instagram account, manage a
         Google Classroom).

    7.  Begin the competition! Start the lessons and guide your members to a winning proposal. Students should be
         able to propose a solution to a problem they experience within their school.
